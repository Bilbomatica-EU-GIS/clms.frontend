Q

How can I find and download the products of the Copernicus Land Monitoring Service?

A

You can find the products by using website search engine or browsing on the website. Each product page contains Map View, Metadata and Download tabs. Go directly to Download tab to download the data. You can only download the data if you are logged in. You can login either by clicking Log in link on the top right of the website or directly on the Download page. After you are logged in you must select files you would like to download. You may be informed that the data you are about to download are not validated yet (in this case you have to accept that). After you click Download selected file(s) an archive with the selected files will be created and you will be offered to download the archive (if you are still on the Download page) and receive e-mail containing the link to the temporary archive (valid for 24hrs). Please note that creating a download archive can take time, depending on the size of the selected files. You can safely leave the Download page and continue exploring the website.

Please note that you also can use Map view to download data. In Map view select your area of interest, click More info and you will be re-directed to the file(s) to download.

Q

I have been trying to register without success. Any idea what could be wrong?

A

On the registration form you must fill in all information that is mandatory. It is also important to tick the ‘I accept the Terms of Use’ checkbox at the bottom of the form. If all this is done correctly, you should receive a welcome message, stating that an e-mail has been sent to you containing a URL that will allow you to set your password. If you don't receive the e-mail please check your spam folder.

Q

I see many different files available in the Download page of a product, but I am not sure which file contains data for the geographical area I am interested in. How can I find out which file covers which geographic area?

A

The products divided into other geographic coverages than the full European coverage (generally the case for the larger data files) always contain a download layer in the web map. When you zoom in and click on the area of interest in the Map View of a product, a pop window will be opened containing a field called DOWNLOAD. If you click on More info, you will be automatically directed to the file to download (if you are not yet logged in you will be asked to log in first).

Q

I can download the large (e.g. 5 GB) DEM files as zipped files, but I cannot unzip them. I can download and unzip smaller files. Is there a specific unzipping application I am supposed to use?

A

You should check whether the used unzip software supports the ZIP64 extensions required for archives larger than 2GB. Please note that Mac OS's default unzip program does not support the ZIP64 extensions.

Q

I have problems downloading large files, it simply stops downloading at some point.

A

Users with slow and unstable internet connection may experience problems downloading large files. We recommend to use a download manager. Simply enter "browser name"+download manager in your search engine to find and download your browser compatible download manager.

Q

I am doing a commercial (for profit) project. What kind of permission do I need to use the data for such purpose? Am I allowed to redisplay the data on a website? Where can I find license information?

A

Access to data is based on a principle of full, open and free access as established by the Copernicus data and information policy Regulation (EU) No 1159/2013 of 12 July 2013. This regulation establishes registration and licensing conditions for GMES/Copernicus users and can be found here: http://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32013R1159.

Free, full and open access to this data set is made on the conditions that:

  1. When distributing or communicating Copernicus dedicated data and Copernicus service information to the public, users shall inform the public of the source of that data and information.
  2. Users shall make sure not to convey the impression to the public that the user's activities are officially endorsed by the Union.
  3. Where that data or information has been adapted or modified, the user shall clearly state this.
  4. The data remain the sole property of the European Union. Any information and data produced in the framework of the action shall be the sole property of the European Union. Any communication and publication by the beneficiary shall acknowledge that the data were produced “with funding by the European Union”.
