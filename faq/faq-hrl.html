Q

Where can I find more information about the HRL’s?

A

The how-to guides section of the portal contains a set of available technical documents for each thematic HRL layer.

Q

What is the update frequency of the different products?

A

The current update frequencies for the HRL’s is three years. This means that the reference years for our products start in 2012 (with the exception of Imperviousness where we have 2006 and 2009 products), and the latest produced reference year is 2015.

Q

What does “reference year” mean in the context of the HRL products?

A

Up to – and including – the 2015 reference year production, we relied heavily on Satellite Imagery by ESA contributing missions (IRS and SPOT mainly for the high resolution (20m) imagery). In particular for northern European countries we often faced the situation of incomplete coverage in the reference year, which is why up to (and including) 2015 reference year actually means +/- 1 year. The result of this was so far, that production could only start at the end of the year following the reference year, which delays availability of data until up to 2-3 years after the reference year.

In the future we will both shorten production time (to around 12 month), and be able to fully cover the area of EEA39 in the actual reference year itself. This is due to dramatically improved image availability using in particular Sentinel 1 and Sentinel 2 satellite data.

Q

Do you have HRL products for download for individual countries?

A

It is planned for the future.

Q

How to interpret a certain Imperviousness value for a pixel?

A

An imperviousness value of e.g. 33% for a certain pixel in the imperviousness product means that we expect 33% of the total area covered by that pixel to be fully sealed. For example a 100 m x 100 m pixel covering 10 000 m2 would be in this case expected to be have around 3 300 m2 of sealed area.
